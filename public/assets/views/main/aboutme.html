Background

Hello! I'm Ivette Sanchez-Perez, a Computer Science student at Fordham University with a strong passion for both technology and the humanities. I’m currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science with a minor in Anthropology. My journey into the tech world began with a love for logic and problem-solving, and over time, I’ve deepened my knowledge through coursework, projects, and hands-on experience. While my major is rooted in technology, my minor in Anthropology reflects my fascination with the world’s diverse cultures and the people that shape them. I enjoy exploring how technology intersects with human behavior and society, giving me a broader and more holistic perspective on the world.

Born and raised in the Bronx, I’ve been fortunate to grow up in a vibrant cultural environment, influenced by my Puerto Rican mother and Dominican father. Fluent in both Spanish and English, I’ve developed a deep appreciation for the diversity of cultures and perspectives around me. This background fuels my passion for learning and adapting across different fields, enabling me to combine my technical expertise with my cultural heritage to approach problems in innovative ways.

Internships

I'm excited to begin my summer internship with Lavner Education at NYU, where I’ll be teaching and mentoring students in technology subjects possibly being coding, robotics, or even web programming. This role allows me to blend my technical knowledge with my passion for education, helping inspire younger learners to explore the creative and logical aspects of technology.

Volunteer Work & Clubs

I’m dedicated to giving back to my community through education. In Highschool I’ve worked as a Spanish Teaching Assistant for a year where I supported students in developing both their Spanish reading and writing skills by regularly checking in and clarifying course materials to make sure everyone was on track. I would also conduct individual and group meetings to help students enhance their Spanish speaking and writing skills and tailoring my assistance to their specific needs.

Additionally, I’ve worked as a Reading tutor for children ages 5-7 in a non-profit organisation called Read Alliance. In this program I had one-on-one sessions with students where I guided students through books to elevate their reading levels to meet educational standards. My duty was to assess and track student progress to ensure students were prepared to advance to more challenging materials.

In addition, I served as Vice President of the Film Club, where I helped organize events, screenings, and discussions around storytelling in cinema. It was a great way to connect with others who share a love for film, while also building leadership and collaboration skills.

Hobbies & Interests

Outside of academics and tech, I’m passionate about creative expression. I love painting, drawing, and working with clay—anything that allows me to bring ideas and imagination to life. These hobbies not only allow me to unwind but also inspire how I approach design in the tech world, influencing my work in user experience and interactive design.

Skills

I’ve worked with a range of programming languages and tools including C++, Java, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and MySQL. I also have experience in full-stack development using PHP, MVC architecture, and have worked with RESTful APIs and database integration into web programs. On top of that, I enjoy creating visual and interactive content, using my skills in design and user experience to build projects that are both functional and engaging.
