Orea

This digital portrait was created on my iPad and features my dog, Orea, who is around 12 years old. Using a reference photo, I added surrounding flowers to give the piece a gentle, sentimental feel. A small butterfly at the tip of her nose adds a playful touch and extra detail.

It’s one of my first attempts at realism and holds a special place in my heart. Not only was this a personal milestone in learning digital art, but it also served as inspiration for a potential tattoo design—something meaningful that would keep Orea with me wherever I go. She’s been a constant companion through my youth and into adulthood, and this piece is a tribute to her presence in my life.

Dream

This art piece is a mixed media piece that combines acrylic painting on canvas with digital elements. If you look closely, you can spot the difference in texture—acrylic paint was used for the buildings, while the water and some surrounding details were added digitally.

This artwork was inspired by a surreal dream I had where the ocean flowed right up to the front steps of random buildings and apartments. Strangely, this wasn’t seen as unusual in the dream, it was simply the way of life, a seamless blend of ocean and everyday living. The vividness of that scene stayed with me, and I sketched it out as soon as I woke up. Since then, I’ve revisited the piece many times, adding new elements like aliens and my dog Orea, gradually transforming it into something deeply personal. Its true meaning is something only I fully understand, which makes it one of my most intriguing and cherished creations.

Valentine FNF

For this Valentine’s Day project, I experimented by combining a square canvas with a circular wooden board. Inspired by the game Friday Night Funkin’, I reimagined myself and my boyfriend as the main characters whose names are simply “Boyfriend” and “Girlfriend” painted onto the wood.

On the background square canvas, I painted a “Highscore” screen where each number holds meaning—for example, “331” represents our special date, March 31st. The large “New Highscore” at the bottom marks our anniversary. This personalized and playful design made for a fun and meaningful artistic expression.

Breakthrough

After water damage created bubbling and peeling on my ceiling and wall, I turned a frustrating situation into an artistic opportunity. I noticed that whenever it rained my ceiling would develop these weird bubbles damaging my celing. At one point it somehow dragged from the ceiling to my wall creating this droopy looking effect. My super then came to repair everything but left the repairs unpainted giving it an unfinished look.

I decided I wanted to take this oppurtunity to paint something on my wall for the first time. I decided to try to make an illusion like painting that makes it look like theres a hole on the wall with something coming out of it. I decided to draw Mangle, a Five Nights at Freddys character, for fun and experimentation, sinces shes kind of an intricate character to draw. I began with a digital mockup, painted the area black, sketched in pencil, and finally added paint. It was my first time working directly on a wall, and it became both an experimental and empowering experience.

Squirrel & Rat

This began as a clay test using a kit from Five Below, but quickly evolved into something more meaningful. I sculpted squirrel and rat figures to represent me and my partner, then painted them after they dried. I decided on these animals specifically was because in the beginning of our relationship we had a special moment with a squirrel that walked up to us and we were able to feed them and rats because we make a lot of jokes about rats and it was one of the animals I used to get into his DMS.

After painting them I noticed they needed some place to stand. I looked around my room for anything I can use and thats when I found small wooden circle discs perfect for this clay work. I added miniature clay flowers and decorative elements to complete the base. What started as a test project became one of my favorite creations—full of personality.

Spiderman Perler

This was one of my most ambitious projects—a gift for my significant other. I wanted to take this oppurtunity to do something different. I wanted to combine perler beads, a 3D frame, comic pages, and painting to create a layered piece centered around Spider-Man.

I searched up large images for a spiderman perler bead design which I found but the picture quality made it hard to see but nevertheless I successfully created the perler bead design. Now it was time to place tape around it to be able to succesfully seperate the beads from the stand whilst keeping the design all together. While doing so I miscalculated and the whole bead design almost completely fell apart. After the disaster I patiently rebuilt it bead by bead before fusing it with an iron. I then assembled a custom background using comic book pages and also placed some pages on the glass and painted spider-sense lines onto the glass for added depth. It’s a mix of creativity, perseverance, and love all in one frame.

The Lobotomy

This was actually my first clay piece that I made while in a Senior Art Project class where the final goal was to present our arts in a Art Show at the end. I wasnt sure what I wanted to focus on but I knew I wanted to focus on clay since at the time it was new to me and I was interested in learning how to use clay efficiently. I also wanted to use a painted cardboard to support the story I wanted to tell.

I took a darker, emotional route—featuring a broken family scene. A male figure sits on a couch, while two children hide behind it. A framed picture shows a happier past, now lost. Scattered clocks represent the loss of time and hope. I wasn’t aiming for a specific message, but I wanted to evoke a sense of emotional weight, uncertainty, and fragmentation.
